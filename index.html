Day by Day

1
Sunday · June 22
Arrive → Edinburgh
Power through the jet lag — straight to the capital
🚂 Glasgow → Edinburgh by train
  • Glasgow Queen Street → Edinburgh Waverley~45–50 min · ~£15–20 pp
    Trains every 30 min. Buy on ScotRail.co.uk or at the station. No booking required — just go!
🏰 Edinburgh Old Town — slow wander
  • The Royal MileFree
    Walk from the Castle Esplanade down to Holyrood Palace. Pubs, whisky shops, and closes (little alleyways) every 30 metres.
  • Victoria StreetFree
    The curved, colourful street said to inspire Diagon Alley. Short but magical — pop in for a wander.
  • GrassmarketFree
    Medieval market square — great spot for a pint and dinner. Relaxed and very atmospheric.
  • National Museum of ScotlandFree
    Huge, fascinating, great for resting tired legs. On Chambers Street.
Save for a return visit: Edinburgh Castle, Arthur's Seat, and Princes Street Gardens are all better done when you're rested. Last trains back to Glasgow run until ~11:45 pm.
2
Monday · June 23
Glasgow — West End & Sights
Necropolis · Kelvingrove · University · Botanics
🏛 Sights
  • Glasgow NecropolisFree
    Highly recommended. Victorian hillside cemetery with sweeping city views. Right next to Glasgow Cathedral — visit both. Eerie and beautiful.
  • Kelvingrove Art Gallery & MuseumFree
    World-class collection in a stunning Spanish Baroque building. Dalí's Christ of Saint John, arms, armour, and much more.
  • University of GlasgowFree
    Stunning Gothic campus in the West End — free to walk through. Great for photos.
  • Botanic GardensFree
    Lovely park along the River Kelvin. The Kibble Palace glasshouse is worth a look inside.
☕ West End Food & Coffee
Paper Cup ☕ Naked Soup 🥣 Focaccia 🍞
All in the West End — easy to hit these between Kelvingrove and the Botanics.
3
Tuesday · June 24
Puffins! Isle of Lunga
Full day · Glasgow → Oban → Mull → Treshnish Isles
🐦 Puffin Day — Book This First!
  • Turus Mara — Treshnish Isles & Staffa Wildlife Tour~£65–75 pp · Book direct
    The gold standard. Family-run, departing Ulva Ferry on Mull. 2+ hours ashore on Lunga with puffins literally at your feet. Also visits Fingal's Cave on Staffa. Runs April–July — June is peak breeding season, the best time to go.
  • Staffa ToursAlternative option
    Departs from Tobermory on Mull. Good option if Turus Mara is full.
🚂 Getting There — Glasgow to Oban
  • Train: Glasgow Queen Street → Oban~2.5 hrs · ~£25–35 pp return
    Depart ~7:30 am to make the morning boat. The Oban line is one of Scotland's great scenic train journeys — go early to get a window seat.
  • Oban → Mull Ferry (CalMac) + Minibus to Ulva Ferry~£10 pp + minibus
    Turus Mara coordinates transfers from Oban — confirm when booking. Return to Glasgow by ~7 pm.
Weather note: Boat tours are weather-dependent and can be cancelled last-minute. Check the forecast the day before. Dress in warm layers even in June — it feels like October on the water.
4
Wednesday · June 25
The Highlands
Glencoe · Loch Lomond · Rannoch Moor · Ben Nevis
🏔 Guided Day Tour from Glasgow
  • Rabbie's — Loch Ness, Glencoe & the Highlands (from Glasgow)From ~£75–85 pp · Book at rabbies.com
    Departs Buchanan Bus Station ~8:30 am, returns ~8:30 pm. Small minibus, max 16 people. Expert storyteller guide. Covers Loch Lomond, Glencoe, Rannoch Moor, Fort Augustus, Loch Ness, and Pitlochry.
🗺 What You'll See
  • Loch Lomond & Luss Village
    Picturesque loch-side village, first stop of the day.
  • Glencoe Valley
    Dramatic and haunting. Site of the 1692 massacre. Also: Bond's Skyfall & Harry Potter filming location.
  • Rannoch Moor
    One of Europe's last great wildernesses — vast, boggy, and extraordinary.
  • Fort William & Ben Nevis
    Charming Highland town at the base of the UK's highest peak. Lunch stop.
  • Loch Ness & Pitlochry
    Optional Loch Ness boat cruise (~£15 extra). Pitlochry is a lovely final Highland stop.
Highland cows: Many Rabbie's guides make a surprise stop for Highland cows — it's a highlight of the tour! Alternatively, Pollok Park in the Glasgow Southside reliably has them and is only 20 min from the city centre.

Friday · June 27
Glasgow Explore
Merchant City · Riverside Museum · Cathedral Quarter
🏙 City Sights
  • Merchant CityFree
    Glasgow's fashionable cultural quarter. Great restaurants, bars, and street art. Built on the wealth of 18th-century tobacco merchants.
  • Glasgow CathedralFree
    Magnificent medieval cathedral, one of Scotland's finest. Right next to the Necropolis if you want a two-for-one.
  • Riverside MuseumFree
    Award-winning transport museum on the Clyde, designed by Zaha Hadid. Spectacular building, brilliant collection.
  • Buchanan Street & Princes SquareFree
    Glasgow's main shopping street. Princes Square is a stunning Victorian shopping centre hidden inside an ornate arcade.
🍽 Evening Dinner
Margo Bar Vini Dishoom
Great evening for a proper sit-down dinner at one of the recommended spots.

Saturday · June 28
Flex Day / Edinburgh Return
Last sights · optional Edinburgh Castle · early packing
🏰 Option A — Edinburgh Return
  • Edinburgh Castle£19.50 adults online · Book at edinburghcastle.scot
    Now you're rested! The castle is spectacular — home of the Honours of Scotland (Crown Jewels). Allow 2+ hours. Note: The Stone of Destiny has permanently moved to Perth Museum. Book online — sells out weeks ahead in summer.
  • Arthur's SeatFree
    The extinct volcano behind Holyrood Palace. A 1-hour hike with panoramic views over Edinburgh and the Firth of Forth. Much better when rested!
  • Edinburgh New TownFree
    Georgian grid streets, Princes Street Gardens, the Scott Monument. A completely different character to the Old Town.
🌿 Option B — Slow Glasgow Morning
  • Slow breakfast, West End wander
    Paper Cup or God Shot for coffee, revisit anything you loved, pack bags.
  • Train to Balloch (Loch Lomond)~45 min · £10 pp
    A peaceful final afternoon at Scotland's most famous loch before your early morning tomorrow.
Tonight: Final dinner out — make it a good one. Set an alarm. Flight is at 11 am, leave hotel by 8:30 am.
